**Persona:** Ravi — a 42-year-old smallholder farmer with 3 acres of mixed land

## SAYS
- "I never know which crop will actually give me a good yield this season."
- "Fertilizer is expensive — I can't afford to waste it on the wrong crop."
- "My neighbor's advice worked for him, but our soil is different."

## THINKS
- "Is there a way to know exactly what my soil needs before I plant?"
- "What if I could test my soil and instantly know the best crop?"
- "I wish someone could tell me this without charging a consultation fee."

## DOES
- Relies on traditional/seasonal knowledge passed down from family
- Occasionally visits the local agriculture office for advice
- Applies fertilizer/water based on estimation rather than measurement

## FEELS
- Anxious about unpredictable yield and income
- Frustrated by lack of accessible expert guidance
- Hopeful that technology could reduce uncertainty and improve profitability

## Key Insight
Ravi doesn't need more raw data — he needs a simple, fast, trustworthy recommendation that
converts his soil and weather readings into one clear decision: **which crop to plant**. This is
exactly the gap OptiCrop is designed to close.
