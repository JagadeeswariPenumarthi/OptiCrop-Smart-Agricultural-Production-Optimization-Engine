## Define Problem Statements (Customer Problem Statement Template)

Farmers often struggle to determine the most suitable crop based on soil nutrients and climate
conditions, which can lead to financial losses and low agricultural yield. The Customer Problem
Statement below captures this challenge from the farmer's point of view.

| Problem Statement (PS) | I am (Customer) | I'm trying to | But | Because | Which makes me feel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PS-1 | A small/marginal farmer deciding what to grow this season | Choose the most profitable and suitable crop for my land | I don't have reliable, data-driven guidance on which crop fits my soil and weather conditions | I lack access to agronomists or soil-testing labs and rely on guesswork or tradition | Anxious about crop failure and financial loss |
| PS-2 | A farmer managing limited water and fertilizer resources | Optimize the use of water, fertilizer, and soil nutrients | I over- or under-apply inputs without knowing the ideal nutrient levels for my crop | There is no easy tool that translates NPK/pH/climate readings into an actionable recommendation | Frustrated by wasted resources and inconsistent yields |
| PS-3 | An agricultural extension officer supporting many farmers | Advise multiple farmers quickly and accurately | Manual, case-by-case assessment doesn't scale across large farmer populations | Data-driven decision support tools are not commonly deployed at the grassroots level | Overwhelmed trying to serve everyone effectively |

## Root Problem Summary
Modern agriculture faces poor crop selection, inefficient resource utilization, changing
environmental conditions, and reduced farming productivity — largely because farmers and
extension workers lack an accessible, intelligent, data-driven system to translate soil and
climate readings into a concrete crop recommendation.
